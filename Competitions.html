Description:
The math competitions are a great way to show an in depth understanding of advanced math concepts. For each contest, there is regional, state, and sometimes a national/international level. Some competitions offer cash prizes and scholarships for thousands of dollars which could benefit a college student. Math competitions focus on the application aspects of math, or applying what you learn in the classroom. Whether you just want to further your understanding in math or you want to win scholarships for college, math contests can help.
The AMC is the American Math Competition, and has different versions - the 8 which is for middle schoolers, and the 10/12 which is for high schoolers. For the high school competition, there is a local, invitational, and national level.
ARML (American Regions Mathematics League) is an annual competition that being together the brightest math students across the nation. It provides an opportunity that allows students to meet, socialize, and compete against one another. The ARML consists of several rounds such as an individual round, a power question (proof-based round), and two relay rounds.
PUMAC math competition is a student-run math competition held at Princeton University. This math competition is meant to be very fun and interactive. It even includes puzzles and math bowl competitions. This is extremely interactive as it it not just a test, but includes many interactive math games and activities.
